P1-A4

Your supplier asked you to pay the $200 they didn't receive

Pillar P1 Short-form 60s · bilingual Topic 06
Research findings (6)
  • Roughly 8 to 15% of SWIFT wires to Chinese suppliers arrive short, delayed, or returned. The two biggest causes: intermediary bank fee deductions and beneficiary name mismatches.
  • Most banks default to SHA (shared fees) in SWIFT field 71A. SHA does not actually mean shared evenly: it means you pay your bank's send fee, the supplier eats every correspondent bank deduction in the chain.
  • Each correspondent bank in the chain deducts $15 to $50 directly from the wire amount before passing it on. The sender is never notified. 1 to 3 hops is typical for a US to China wire, so on a $50,000 wire a supplier can lose $30 to $150 invisibly, sometimes more.
  • Real reference: a Vienna company paid $12,000 to a US vendor using SHA. Vendor received $11,940. The $60 vanished into the correspondent chain with no line item.
  • The fix is one of two things. Either change the SWIFT instruction to OUR (sender pays all chain fees, supplier gets full amount, costs the sender $30 to $75 more upfront) or bypass the SWIFT chain entirely via CIPS or MYbank routing, where there is no intermediary chain to deduct from.
  • China-bound wires also fail on beneficiary name mismatch. A 5 to 10% return rate in the China corridor traces back to pinyin vs Chinese character name, missing 'Co., Ltd.', or wrong company-form suffix on the SWIFT MT103. Returns cost 2 to 3 weeks of delay and another round of fees.

How to read FX margin off your wire confirmation

Pillar P2 Short-form 60s tutorial Topic 02
Research findings (4)
  • Reading FX margin from a wire confirmation requires three numbers: applied rate (on the confirmation), interbank rate (Google/XE/Reuters at the same time), and wire amount.
  • Formula: ((Interbank rate - Applied rate) / Interbank rate) x 100 = margin percent. Multiply by wire amount = silent cost.
  • Example: applied 7.05, interbank 7.22 at 10:15am same day. Spread = (7.22-7.05)/7.22 x 100 = 2.35 percent. On $50K = $1,175.
  • Most SEA bank wire confirmations include the applied rate but never list the mid-market reference, by design.
